About the hatchery

A conservation initiative called the Bentota Turtle Hatchery, sometimes called the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, is situated in Bentota, Sri Lanka. It serves as both a critical hub for sea turtle preservation and rehabilitation and a well-liked tourist destination. The Olive Ridley, Green, Hawksbill, and Loggerhead sea turtles, as well as other species that may be found in the area, are the main focus of the hatchery. In their native environments, these turtle species are threatened in a number of ways, including egg theft, pollution, habitat deterioration, and unintentional fishing net capture. To overcome these difficulties and guarantee the survival of these exquisite creatures, the hatchery was established.

The conservation activities at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery start with the gathering of turtle eggs from threatened nesting locations along the coast. To mimic the circumstances of a natural nest, the gathered eggs are then carefully placed in sandy hatcheries inside the facility. By doing thus, they are shielded from potential predators and outside intervention. The young turtles are raised in specially constructed tanks until they are strong enough to be returned into the ocean when the eggs hatch. Those who visit the hatchery may watch the eggs being laid and even help release the hatchlings into the water. It's a rare chance to aid in the preservation of these threatened animals.

Additionally, the hatchery significantly contributes to educating both the local populace and tourists about sea turtle conservation. In order to underline the value of maintaining these lovely species and marine habitats, they hold educational programs, seminars, and lectures. Tourists may view and learn about sea turtles while also actively contributing to conservation efforts by going to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. To continue operating and conserving these threatened species, the hatchery depends on donations and admission fees. Overall, Sri Lanka's efforts to conserve, save, and release sea turtles are greatly aided by the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. It offers a special chance for guests to interact with these amazing animals while encouraging their long-term survival.

What you can do at the hatchery

Here are a bunch of things you can do at this Turtle Hatchery.

  • You can pet injured sea turtles who are being treated in their tanks.
  • You can release baby turtles into the sea
  • You can sign up to volunteer or be a patron by donating to this great cause
